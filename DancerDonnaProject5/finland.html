Finland Archipelago

Finland is a country in northern Europe with a population of of 5.4 million people. In 1917, Finland gained its independence from Russia and
prior to that was part of Sweden from the 12th to 19th century. During WWII, Finland was able to maintain its independence, defending against
invasions from Germany and the Soviet Union. Finland's terrain is low, hilly, and 70% forested. Finland has over 60,000 lakes.
